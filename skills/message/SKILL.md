Use $ARGUMENTS to specify the occasion or paste the context. If unclear, ask one question: "What do you want to communicate and to whom?"

**Occasion types and approach:**

- **Event or special** — key detail first (what, when, why it's worth coming), then a single clear call to action
- **VIP invitation** — personal, exclusive tone. Never mass-blast language. "We're holding a table for you" beats "Come visit us"
- **Post-visit follow-up** — warm, brief. No ask for a 5-star review — it's tacky. Just genuine appreciation + something specific if possible
- **Apology or incident** — acknowledge directly, don't over-explain, offer something concrete if appropriate
- **Booking reminder** — date, time, party size, contact to cancel. Nothing else.
- **Daily special or promotion** — lead with the offer or dish, not the brand. One thing, not a list.

**Rules for every message:**
- Under 3 sentences unless the occasion has logistics
- No emojis unless the restaurant brand uses them
- Address by first name if known, nothing if not (no "Dear Customer")
- One exclamation mark maximum per message
- Sound like a person who knows you, not a marketing department
