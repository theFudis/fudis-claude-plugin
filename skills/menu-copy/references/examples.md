## Banned words (universally)
`delicious` ┬Ě `tasty` ┬Ě `juicy` ┬Ě `exquisite` ┬Ě `homemade` (unless actually made on-site) ┬Ě `fresh` (too generic) ┬Ě `amazing`

**Why:** These words occupy space that could be used for actual sensory or technique information. Future diners trust specificity, not adjectives.

## Pricing framing
- Non-staple items: avoid round numbers ending in 0 (38 reads better than 40)
- Premium items: price to signal quality, not to compete
- Don't anchor the cheapest item far below the average ÔÇö it creates cognitive dissonance about the restaurant's tier
- Prix-fixe: the value is *clarity + curation*, not discount ÔÇö frame it as an experience, not a deal
