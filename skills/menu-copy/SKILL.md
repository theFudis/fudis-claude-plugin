Use $ARGUMENTS to specify: a dish name to write copy for, a menu section to restructure, or "review" to audit the full menu copy if items are provided.

**For writing a dish description:**
Ask for: dish name, main ingredients, cooking method, and any story or origin if relevant.

Good menu copy:
- 1–2 sentences max — menus are scanned, not read
- Leads with the most compelling element (texture, origin, technique, or occasion — not a list of ingredients)
- Evokes appetite without being cliché — see `references/examples.md` for the banned-words list
- Matches the restaurant's voice — casual bistro ≠ fine dining

For culturally specific transformation examples across cuisines, load `references/examples.md`.

**For pricing framing:**
Anchor around the restaurant's own average ticket — ask if unknown. Avoid round numbers ending in 0 for non-staple items. Price premium items to signal quality, not to compete on value.

**For menu section restructuring:**
Order sections by how guests actually eat (starters → mains → desserts, or local equivalent). Name sections with personality only if the brand can carry it. Group by occasion or ingredient family, not just by protein.

Match the voice, cuisine, and cultural register of the restaurant throughout.
