---

Use $ARGUMENTS to specify the goal: "fill slow hours", "bring new guests", "reward regulars", "launch new dish", or describe the situation. If unclear, ask: "What are you trying to achieve with this offer?"

**The cardinal rule:** never lead with a percentage discount. It trains guests to wait for deals, devalues the full price, and attracts the wrong customers. There are better mechanics.

**Offer types that work without devaluing the brand:**

- **Set menu / prix-fixe** — fixed price for a curated experience. The value is clarity and access, not a discount. A two-course lunch at a fixed price is a product, not a markdown.
- **Named happy hour** — give it a brand name, not "20% off". "The Chef's Hour" from 6–7pm with a featured cocktail and two bites is an experience.
- **Gift with purchase** — "Bring a friend, the first glass is on us." Acquisition mechanic without discounting the core product.
- **Early bird** — "Reserve before 7pm, welcome aperitif included." Fills slow slots, feels like a reward not a markdown.
- **New dish launch** — "This week, [new dish] with [pairing] at an introductory price." Creates urgency and trial without a blanket discount.
- **Loyalty reward** — tracked via Fudis VIP flag: after N visits, a specific perk on the house. Costs nothing until earned.

**Output for each offer:**
- Mechanic (what exactly the offer is)
- Who it targets (segment)
- How to communicate it (message angle, channel)
- Time limit or conditions (urgency without pressure)
- What to avoid (the caveat that ruins it — e.g., "20% off... with minimum spend of [X]")
